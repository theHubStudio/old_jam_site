C:/satbayev/dev.hub/about

Retro game Jam from Satbayev University and the Hub community is a three-day competition and exhibition from independent game developers from all over the city of Almaty, dedicated to creating games! The event will be held offline, and works are accepted only from those who participated in the official site. GameJam will take place in team mode, so come with friends or find a team right on the jam!

    What is awaiting you:
  • Free participation
  • Help from specialists
  • Useful workshops and lectures from professionals
  • 30 hours for development
  • 6 Nominations to Win
  • Interesting dating
  • Show your game to the public
  • Interesting prizes

Also, everything that will make these 3 days unforgettable! New friends, community support, crazy ideas, incredible boost to your skills, last minute upload and playthrough from many other developers from Almaty! The goal of our GameJam is to build a strong community of game developers in our country! See you soon.

C:/satbayev/dev.hub/faq

+ What should I do if I don't have a team?

- You can apply to participate alone, either assemble a team on site, or join already existing teams before the start of development. However, if you fail to find the command, you will not be able to take part in the competition, but you will be able to take part in lectures and workshops.

+ Our team has no experience at all, what should we do?

- GameJam is a great way to get the first experience, after all, everything happens for the first time!

+ Can we use Unity3D/RPG Maker/Game Maker or any other game engine in our project development?

- Yes, you can use any game engine as long as the final project is available on the platforms specified in the GameJam rules.

+ Does the project have to correspond to the "Retro" theme?

- Yes. This is a necessary limitation to encourage your creativity.

+ Do we need to bring our own equipment (Laptops, tablets, etc.)?

- Yes, you need to come to Game Jam with your own equipment.

C:/satbayev/dev.hub/rules

  • The project must correspond to the theme of the Game Jam, in this case - "Retro". However, the interpretation of the topic remains with the participants.
  • Development time - 48 hours
  • PC development platform (Windows / Linux / MacOs systems) or Web.
  • Mandatory attendance at registration and closing of the event.
  • Teams from 2 to 5 people can participate.
  • Templates or previously created prototypes are allowed if they have been created by contributors.
  • Any development technologies are allowed.
  • Games should be based on a theme, and there should also be things in the project that refer to 3 of the 6 set additional themes (themes are opened immediately before the start of the jam)
  • All publicly available libraries and middleware are allowed
  • All content creation and developer utilities allowed (3dsmax, Photoshop, Blender, etc.)
  • Source code must be attached to the game
  • Infringement of intellectual property and copyright in participants' projects is strictly prohibited and may result in disqualification, all assets used by non-contributors must be free for non-commercial use or you must have written permission to use them!
  • Team interaction is unlimited.
